# my-project

Defence or Empathy? A Digital Contrastive Study of Ku Hung-ming and Lin Yutang’s Construction of the Chinese National Character
 
Lingnan University | MA in Digital History in Global Asia | Digital Humanities Project
 
Project Overview
 
This project uses corpus‑based discourse analysis to quantitatively compare Ku Hung‑ming’s The Spirit of the Chinese People (1915) and Lin Yutang’s My Country and My People (1935). By measuring word frequency, chapter distribution, and we/they pronoun ratio, the study empirically verifies the long‑standing qualitative claim that Ku adopted a defensive strategy while Lin used an empathetic approach in constructing the image of Chinese national character for Western readers.
 
Key Findings
 
- Ku Hung‑ming: High frequency of abstract terms (moral / civilisation), 70.4% concentrated in Chapter 1, we/they ratio = 0.71 → defensive discourse
- Lin Yutang: High frequency of concrete terms (Chinese / people), even distribution of moral vocabulary, we/they ratio = 2.7 → empathetic discourse
