# Defence or Empathy? A Digital Contrastive Study of Ku Hung-ming and Lin Yutang’s Construction of the Chinese National Character

Lingnan University | MA in Digital History in Global Asia | Digital Humanities Capstone Project

## Project Overview
This project uses corpus-based discourse analysis to quantitatively compare Ku Hung-ming’s *The Spirit of the Chinese People* (1915) and Lin Yutang’s *My Country and My People* (1935). 

By measuring word frequency, chapter lexical distribution, and we/they pronoun positioning ratio, this study empirically verifies the long-standing qualitative academic claim: Ku Hung-ming adopted a defensive discourse strategy, while Lin Yutang utilised an empathetic narrative approach when constructing and presenting the Chinese national character to Western international readers.

## Key Findings
- **Ku Hung-ming**: High frequency of abstract moral/civilisational terms (`moral` / `civilisation`). 70.4% of core moral vocabulary concentrated in the opening chapter. We/they pronoun ratio = 0.71 → typical defensive discourse pattern
- **Lin Yutang**: High frequency of concrete identity-focused terms (`Chinese` / `people`). Moral vocabulary distributed evenly across all chapters. We/they pronoun ratio = 2.7 → typical empathetic, inclusive discourse pattern
