Defence or Empathy?

A Digital Contrastive Study of Chinese National Character
Ku Hung‑ming vs Lin Yutang

How did two leading Chinese intellectuals portray their own people to the West? Ku Hung‑ming (1915) built a moral fortress – dense abstract vocabulary, defensive stance, distant from the reader. Lin Yutang (1935) built a bridge of everyday empathy – concrete nouns, reader‑inclusive pronouns, inductive structure.

This project uses computational text analysis (word frequency, chapter distribution, pronoun ratio) to reveal measurable differences behind a long‑noticed contrast. The website visualises the evidence and offers a digital companion to the MA thesis.
